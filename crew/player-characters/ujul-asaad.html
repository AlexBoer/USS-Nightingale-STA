Adsog LogStardate 58191 - Nightingale Log 11.2 - Abyss Station
As a doctor you treat the comrade in front of you. Not the one you wish you had.

This comrade was weird and annoying.

I knew as part of the exchange I would need to come equipped with the “open mind” they called it.

I am dedicated to my duty. I preserve honor and balance it with life and death. I do this for the patient and for The Empire. But opening this patient’s mind enough to comprehend life and honor was a chore.

The patients “empire” has a death wish and the patient didn’t seem to understand if he was alive or dead despite all experience and medical evidence.

I am perfectly willing to preserve a patients choice to find an honorable death or seek glory in life. Even if I do not understand their motivation or even believe they will achieve glory. Live and prove me wrong. Thank me later.

But how am I supposed to respect your insane death cult if you dont? Convince me you want to die. Convince me you want to live. Choose or the choice is made for you.

Meanwhile, despite my efforts to respect and fortify his convictions it seemed like Star Fleet was perfectly happy to talk him in circles until he came around to choosing life. The hypocrisy.

I know my people value honor more than most. But I do not think others are without the concept. Still their willingness to risk a life without honor is troubling. If Akarra ever regrets his choice he will have to live his days with the belief that he ruined his people. What kind of life is that to save? Were they really so afraid of death, not even their own death, that they might inflict that fate upon another?

The patient gave his support for the Captain’s decision to transport his entire people away from their believed ascension. To accept life. -But not for himself. He considers himself “already dead.” Currently. Maybe. I don't know. His mind is weak.

 I admit I am relieved at his choice though. Based on the Captain’s actions he would not have allowed Akkara to be transported to his world. Nor would he have listened if Akkara insisted we leave his people to their fate.

It would be troublesome to question the Captains authority. I’m sure star fleet has a process for this that would take a million times longer to solve the issue than just killing him would. But even if this were an Empire ship I’m not sure I could have killed him fast enough to belay his order.

Either way. The cultists are a fascinating species and culture. Their nanites while not borg are…unusual. I have obtained a sample to study. The officers do not seem concerned, and I am here to learn what they have. This could be quite interesting. Even if the application proves appalling, knowing and understanding it will be of use.

As for the patient I have discharged him. He is physically sound and perhaps after bickering with his people his convictions will be as well.  > In the meantime I will continue to respect his (philosophical?) belief he is dead. This time, when I hold the tea ceremony he is invited to join. It is not often one can speak to their dead patient. I hope he attends.
