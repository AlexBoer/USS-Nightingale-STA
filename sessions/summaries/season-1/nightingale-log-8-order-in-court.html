Nightingale Log 8 - Order in Court

Summary

Aram played Loxor the Ferengi prosecutor (non-Starfleet, most of Starfleet didn't want to touch the trial) and a guest star played Captain Ojok Tokmoth the Klingon defense lawyer Additional details learned during session:

  • Stoker was a Rear Admiral
  • The other Admiral in charge of Narendra Station was Admiral Opok Krakt (she/her), it's a joint Federation/Klingon operation
  • After stealing artifact, Stoker was on another ship exploring planets, encountered trouble, fled to Nightingale while being chased by USS Walcott
  • The artifact was stored in a special Starfleet box for safety until incident with Orions, only recently been out to be studied.

The judge was Admiral Jackson Nolan (human, he/him).

Before trial, Stoker was talking to Tokmoth in a secure area/meeting room for privacy. Explains attorney/client privilege, Stoker doubts them, Tokmoth invokes Klingon honor, Stoker says not like she has a choice anyway. Tokmoth says Stoker has right to challenge them to a duel if defense not good.

Opening arguments: Loxor opening argument was centered on General Orders 17 and 29 about not unnecessarily endangering Starfleet personnel or the Federation. Tokmoth opening argument is that it's our duty to explore but that we don't control what we find (invoked Prime Directive as an example) and that it's important fill in gaps with assumptions. Tokmoth says duty is to explore single question - did admiral stoker display both intent and ability to carry out crimes accused of.

Review of facts, then lunch. Loxor interviewed Lt. Relza, who was against Stoker and saw no medical evidence for influence. Tokmoth talked further with Admiral Stoker, found out she considered herself a whistle-blower and that the Federation were exploiting the Tilikaal for their technology.

Captain Zhao took the stand, asked questions by both sides. Loxor tried to build a case that the Tilikaal are an existential threat to the Federation. Tokmoth tried to build a case that it was Starfleet's duty to interact diplomatically with a species asking for help and that hostility and motivations can be difficult to interpret in unfamiliar cultures.

Tokmoth spoke to Lt. Yalis, Yalis explained that Admiral doesn't have an ancient Tilikaal in her but does Yalis said Admiral used to be more cautious and that behavior changed after contact with artifact. Yalis also thinks Lt. Gray might have been mind-controlled into his stunt with the transporters and the ghosts.

An initial interaction between Tokmoth and Algan Kader happened, can't remember if it was on the stand or asking questions.

Out of courtroom, Yalis investigated artifact. Found it was broadcasting a wide/untargeted subspace signal that was probably a distress call. Also used a medical testing hologram to discover that it could have subtle effects on the brain that might affect thoughts/behavior. Yalis told Tokmoth about this. Got permission to use scanner on Admiral Stoker, Admiral Stoker's brain showed effects.

Admiral Stoker took the stand, questioned by both Loxor first. If free and given a ship, she would go to a facility the Tilikaal told her about. Tried to establish a case that Stoker was serving the Tilikaal. Then Tokmoth asked questions - Admiral does not believe what she wants to do would endanger the Federation.

During Admiral Stoker's questions with Tokmoth, Algan Kader noticed thoughts in the room. Admiral Krakt was angry the court martial was questioning her friend Admiral Stoker's honor. Admiral Nolan (the judge) was thinking lyrics to "Twinkle twinkle little star" to block mind-reading, on closer look Kader heard him think "I have to convict her and remove her from the picture" (referring to Admiral Stoker).

Ensign Kader told Yalis he needed a disturbance so he could talk to Tokmoth. Yalis started some dyke drama with Relza and it escalated into punching, arrested, session went to recess. Kader told Tokmoth about what he heard in the judge's thoughts.

When court reconvened, Tokmoth called Kader to the stand, and had him reveal what he had heard in the judge's thoughts. Admiral Krakt had Klingon security on the station arrest the judge, and a mistrial was declared.

After the trial, Admiral Stoker resigned her commission, is now a civilian. Loxor and Tokmoth talked after court, acknowledged each-other as worthy adversaries, might even work together in the future. Tokmoth also talked to Ensign Kader and commended his bravery in coming forward and said helping cowards find their bravery to create a more honorable galaxy is why they became a lawyer.

If Yalis is charged for the punch-up in the courtroom, Tokmoth will defend her.
